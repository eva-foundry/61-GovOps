# GovOps Partner Program

A framework for building a global network of implementation partners, training providers, and domain experts around GovOps.

---

## Why a Partner Ecosystem?

GovOps is open source. Any organization can use it. But governments adopting GovOps need:

- **Implementers** who understand both the technology and the legislative context
- **Trainers** who can prepare officers and analysts to use the system
- **Domain experts** who can encode complex legislation accurately
- **Integrators** who can connect GovOps to existing government systems

No single organization can serve every jurisdiction, every language, every legal tradition. The partner ecosystem scales this.

---

## Partner Tiers

### Community Partner (no requirements)
- Anyone contributing to the open-source project
- Access to community discussion and support
- Can use GovOps freely under Apache 2.0
- Listed in the community contributors page

### Registered Implementer
- At least 1 completed Phase 0 engagement (validated)
- At least 2 staff with GovOps certifications
- Published case study (anonymized if needed)
- Committed to contributing jurisdiction data back to open source
- **Benefits**: listed on partner directory, implementation support channel, early access to features

### Certified Training Provider
- At least 1 instructor with GCA (Certified Architect) credential
- Uses approved training curriculum
- Delivers at least 2 training cohorts per year
- Maintains student satisfaction ratings
- **Benefits**: listed on training directory, curriculum updates, co-marketing

### Strategic Partner
- Multiple completed implementations across jurisdictions
- Active contributor to the GovOps core project
- Demonstrated thought leadership (publications, conference presentations)
- **Benefits**: governance advisory role, roadmap input, joint case studies

## Geographic Opportunities

GovOps is jurisdiction-aware by design. Every country with rule-based government programs is a potential market.

**High-opportunity regions**:

| Region | Why | Example Programs |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| **Post-conflict reconstruction** | Need to rebuild institutional systems from legislation | Pension, IDP assistance, property registration |
| **EU accession candidates** | Must harmonize with EU acquis; need transparent systems | Social benefits, regulatory compliance |
| **Developing economies** | Digitizing government services for the first time | Tax, permits, social protection |
| **OECD countries** | Modernizing legacy systems; audit/accountability pressure | Pension, immigration, healthcare |
| **Federal systems** | Multiple jurisdictions with overlapping rules | Benefits, tax, environmental regulation |

## How to Join

1. **Start contributing** to the open-source project (add a jurisdiction, fix bugs, improve docs)
2. **Get certified** (individual certifications are free during the founding period)
3. **Complete a Phase 0 engagement** (can be self-funded as a demonstration project)
4. **Publish a case study** (even a small one — the first movers get the most visibility)
5. **Apply for Registered Implementer status** via GitHub

## Open-Source Economics

The GovOps model is "give away the method, sell the implementation":

- **The engine is free** — governments can inspect it, no license fees
- **The rules are contributed back** — each new jurisdiction benefits everyone
- **The implementation is valuable** — every government context is different
- **The training is ongoing** — legislation changes, staff turns over

This is the model that built Red Hat, WordPress, and Kubernetes. The rising tide lifts all boats.
